What Toronto's open data shows is happening on Mimico Ave and Stanley Ave.

The City of Toronto installs driver-feedback radar signs called "Watch Your Speed" along streets it has identified as safety zones. Every vehicle that passes is logged. Two of those signs sit on streets where many of us live — one on Mimico Avenue at #30, one on Stanley Avenue across from #148. Both are 30 km/h zones. Both have a former Automated Speed Enforcement camera within 300 m. Here is what the data shows for those two signs, comparing the first four months of 2026 against the same window in 2025.

The wider context

The Province of Ontario banned Automated Speed Enforcement cameras across the province in November 2025. Both of the locations above sat within 300 m of a former ASE camera. Across the city, an analysis of all 1,200-plus Watch Your Speed signs shows the share of vehicles measured at 10+ km/h over the limit rose roughly 10% between the same windows — and rose more on streets that sat closest to former ASE cameras. The local pattern on Mimico Ave and Stanley Ave is consistent with that wider city pattern.

No causal claim is being made here. There are factors other than camera removal that could shift traffic on any specific street — construction, school terms, weather, neighbourhood changes. What's reported above is what the City of Toronto's own monthly counts on the two streets show: more vehicles measured well above the posted 30 km/h limit in the first four months of 2026 than in the same window the previous year, on the same two signs.
